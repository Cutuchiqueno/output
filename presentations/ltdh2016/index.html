State of Digital Annotations

  • W3C Open Annotation and Web Annotation

  • research field specific community projects
  • The topic of annotations has raised significant interest in the field of Digital Humanities and beyond during the last years. Many _ESFRI_projects from the Humanities or Social Sciences have worked on annotations in corresponding working packages. With _the pund.it_ _EUROPEANA_ has also built its own annotation tool. PELAGIOS Infrastructure around annotations.

All these activities have significantly multiplied the usage and usage scenarios of annotations.How do these activities affect the understanding of annotations or the ways of annotating?

The Hierarchy Between Annotated Object and Annotation

Annotating, the act of creating associations between distinct pieces of information.

Sanderson et al. 2013

  • A traditional way to look at annotations implies a hierarchical relationship between the annotation and the object that is annotated. This relationship is visually well illustrated in medieval glosses which are often arranged around the text in the center of the page. The relationship exists also on the level of production. A book is produced for the main text and gives reason to add annotations 'in the margins'.
  • In definition of the concept of annotations within the Open Annotation Data Model this relationship vanishes. It says:
    Annotating, the act of creating associations between distinct pieces of information.
  • The formal semantics still contain the concept of body and target but technically and methodologically there is no necessary difference in the way Open Annotation understands the relationship between body and target.

The Interchangeability Between Body and Target

  • In the _Pelagios_ project for instance sources are annotated with data about places. However, the services Pelagios provide completely blur this dependency. If the places are annotations to the texts or the other way around depends on one's point of interest or technically spoken on the interface.

Annotations as First-Class Research-Publications

  • This has to do with another principle of historical annotations that becomes more and more fragile: the existential dependency of annotations from the annotated object. Annotations exist on the paper of books and vanishes away with it. Digital annotations can be physically stored and disseminated independently. That means annotation data is a primary research output in itself and not solely anymore a documentation of the path that was taken to these results. By using Pelagios, annotations are brought to the level of first class research results.

The Temporal Precedence of the Annotated Object

  • Roorda introduces another interesting prospect. In his _Shebanq_ project [@roorda2016shebanq] he uses annotations for queries. In this scenario the annotated object is the volatile entity. The target of the annotation might look differently each time the annotation is rendered. The stable unit is the query as an interface, that means the representation of a certain way to look at things.

DARIAH Working Group Questionnaire

  • Next, I will present some of the results from the aforementioned questionnaire that was carried out in the DARIAH WG. With the questionnaire we wanted to evaluate context dimensions in annotating which are constitutive both to identify existing annotation practices as well as metadata needs for the publication of annotation data.

The Fabrication of the Annotated Object

  • The first aspects which influences the appropriate interpretation of the meaning in annotations concerns the technological production of the annotated object. In the use-case about the audio-visual laboratory archive the participant remarks that knowledge about the process of ethnographic film making is very supportive to understand the annotations about these films. Likewise, the _Monasterium_ use-case reveals that certain annotations on documents can only be understood with knowledge about the creation of digital copies of these documents.

The Fragment Dimension

  • Another dimension does also concern the annotated object. However, this time it is about the question what is technically referenced. The _Video Annotation in Transcultural Studies_ (VATS) as well as the _Semantic Topological Notes_ (SemToNotes) use-case emphasize that many annotation services do not offer the possibility to exactly reference a shape in an image. Instead, the annotation reference creates a box around the shape of interest. The possibility to exactly know and to technically describe the part of an object is a challenging issue.

Facets and Modalities Behind the Surface of Annotated Objects

  • The fragment dimension is part of a bigger issue. This issue is about the facet which is addressed by the annotation. Some examples will clarify what is meant. The _Relations in Space_ use-case in which inscriptions in Jewish gravestones are annotated distinguishes between annotations about the carrier of the inscription (the gravestone) and the inscription itself. Accordingly, annotations which reference the same area in the digital copy introduce completely different angles. Likewise an annotation can also address linguistic, visual, spatial or other aspects or modalities.

Transparency About the Semantics in the Annotation

  • Knowledge about structure and properties of annotations is one thing, knowledge about concepts which are used in annotations are another. Certainly, this issue is well discussed in the Semantic Web domain and a lot of annotation data is produced following the Semantic Web compliant cause of conduct. Nonetheless, this issue can be more complicated and numerous examples in the survey give evidence about this fact.
  • For instance tThe 'e-Poetics' use-case applies a technical terminology which complies with the rules of literature study. The issue is that no technological representation for this terminology exist.
  • The example of e-Metaphor is even more complicated. E-Metaphor's concept of metaphors is defined very precisely for the purpose of the project. In this case it is the peculiarity of the definition linking to a specific type of theory of metaphors which complicates the appropriate use of annotations and which might create misunderstandings.

The Dialogic Aspect of Annotations

  • The DWork use-case highlights another interesting dimension of annotations. The participants remark that the meaning of annotations is often shaped by relations between annotations of the same annotating process. Renata Agosti called this once very nicely the 'dialogic' aspect of annotations. Accordingly, annotations should always contain information which make it possible to dereference corresponding annotations.

    • An Environmental Approach to Interoperability

    • interoperability is not just formal semantics, it is also processes, human actions and shared understanding
    • interoperability should be designed more by strategic publishing and communication goals of researchers
  • Thus the information needs of researchers and even of computers do not always require technically serialized formal semantics. This does not invalidate the whole approach I have presented here. The innovations and ubiquity of annotations demand create a holistic approach. However, the complexity such an approach creates demands to become pragmatic again. Butthe crucial point is, what pragmatic means. If we put the whole topic into the frame of semantic interoperability which it partially is than there are there 2 approaches: the Dublin Core and the CIDOC approach. The first defines pragmatic in terms of finding the lowest common semantic denominator to create a global space of interoperability. The second defines pragmatic from the perspective of the researcher or publisher. He or she decides what to make formally explicit and how to align to the CIDOC reference model. Thus the process of making something semantically interoperable is guided by pragmatic decisions in relation to the environment the researcher address and his or her communicatve goals.
  • Disciplines like Computer Science and Informations often focus on the first approach. However, as Humanist Researchers and corresponding to the complexity of the annotation topic we should develop further the second one. And discussions that are taking place at the moment under the terms of Minimal Computing or Post-Digitality show that this approach is in fact becoming more influentila on a broader scale.
  • In this respect the systematology is not just meant to create more transparent metadata around annotation data. It was also created to be a conceptual tool for a better understanding of current annotation practices, a more efficient design of annotation data publication scenarios and a context aware use of published annotation data.

    • The second answer to this question I partially gave already at the beginning.     • But the ubiquity of annotations does not only mean that annotating may be applied everywhere.
